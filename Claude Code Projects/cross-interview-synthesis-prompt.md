- A pivot from building automation pilots to extended discovery

## STEP 2: IDENTIFY CROSS-INTERVIEW PATTERNS

Analyze all transcripts together and extract insights in these areas:

### 1. Recurring Themes

For each theme, identify:
- **Theme name**: Clear, specific description
- **Frequency**: Mentioned by X of Y interviewees
- **Consistency**: Do people describe it the same way or differently?
- **Representative quotes**: 2-3 quotes from different people illustrating the theme
- **Strength**: Is this a clear consensus or a weak signal?

Focus on themes related to:
- Skill & knowledge gaps
- Process inconsistencies
- Definitions of "good work"
- Automation vs training perspectives
- Any other emergent themes related to the training validation objective

### 2. Stakeholder Perspective Mapping

Map out how different groups/individuals view the situation:
- **By role**: What do PMs say vs what do leaders say?
- **By team**: Are there team-specific patterns? (Josh's team vs Frank's team vs others)
- **By seniority/experience level**: Do more experienced PMs see things differently?

For each perspective cluster:
- What's their primary concern/focus?
- What language/terminology do they use?
- What solutions do they gravitate toward?

### 3. Contradictions & Tensions

Identify where people disagree or where evidence conflicts:
- What do some people say that others contradict?
- Where do individual perspectives clash with the group narrative?
- What assumptions does one person make that another challenges?
- Are there "fault lines" that might affect solution adoption?

### 4. What Good Looks Like (Consensus vs Divergence)

Synthesize across all definitions of "good work":
- Is there consensus on quality criteria? Or wide variation?
- Where do people align on what makes a good PRD?
- Where do they diverge?
- What does this variance tell us about the need for standardization vs training?

### 5. Evidence for Training vs Automation vs Hybrid

Synthesize what the collective evidence suggests:

**Training Indicators**:
- Patterns suggesting knowledge/skill gaps that training could address
- Examples of inconsistency that standardized capability building could solve
- Situations where people want to understand "why" not just "how"

**Automation Indicators**:
- Patterns suggesting repetitive work that could be automated
- Examples where AI could eliminate manual effort
- Situations where people want efficiency over learning

**Hybrid Indicators**:
- Patterns suggesting both are needed
- Examples where automation enables better human judgment
- Situations where AI augments capability rather than replacing it

**Ambiguous/Insufficient Evidence**:
- What remains unclear
- Where we need more data
- Contradictory signals

### 6. Emergent Insights

What do you learn by looking at ALL interviews that you couldn't see from individual conversations?
- Meta-patterns about the organization
- Systemic issues vs individual challenges
- Cultural factors affecting solution viability
- Unexpected connections between themes

## STEP 3: SYNTHESIS SUMMARY

Provide a comprehensive summary (500-750 words) answering:

1. **What's the strongest signal?** What do we know with high confidence?

2. **What's the primary recommendation?** Based on the evidence, does this lean toward training, automation, or hybrid? Why?

3. **What's the primary risk?** What could make the recommended solution fail?

4. **What stakeholder dynamics matter?** Who needs to be aligned? Where's resistance likely?

5. **What questions remain unanswered?** What do we still need to discover?

6. **What's the narrative?** If you had to tell a story about what's happening at Asurion's PM organization based on these interviews, what would it be?

---

## STEP 4: QUOTABLE EVIDENCE

Identify the 5-8 most powerful quotes across all interviews that:
- Illustrate the key themes
- Could be used in stakeholder presentations
- Represent different perspectives
- Are concrete and specific (not generic platitudes)

For each quote, note:
- Who said it (role/context if not identifying)
- What theme it supports
- Why it's compelling

---

## OUTPUT FORMAT

Use clear headings, bullet points, and tables where appropriate. Make it scannable for quick insights but detailed enough to support decision-making. Bold key findings. Include specific numbers (e.g., "5 of 7 interviewees mentioned...").
